Preparing for Independent Practice in Canada: A Resident Physiotherapist's Journey

February 2025  ·  4 min read  ·  By Aditya Musunury

The transition toward independent physiotherapy practice in Canada is both professional and personal. It involves licensure milestones, adaptation to a new care system, and continued growth in communication, documentation, and clinical reasoning.

For resident physiotherapists, supervised practice offers an important opportunity to build confidence while aligning prior experience with local expectations and standards.

This process is not only about examinations. It is also about developing reflective practice, consistency, accountability, and patient-centered decision-making in real clinical settings.

Every patient interaction adds to readiness for independent practice. Growth happens through observation, feedback, self-reflection, and practical application.

The journey toward independent practice reflects ongoing learning, adaptation, and commitment to high-quality rehabilitation care.

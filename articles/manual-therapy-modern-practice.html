Manual Therapy in Modern Physiotherapy Practice

March 2025  ·  6 min read  ·  By Aditya Musunury

Manual therapy can be a useful part of physiotherapy when applied with clear purpose and integrated into a broader rehabilitation plan.

It may help with pain modulation, short-term mobility improvement, and movement confidence. However, it is most effective when combined with active treatment such as exercise and education.

Modern physiotherapy no longer treats manual therapy as a stand-alone solution. Sustainable outcomes are more likely when hands-on techniques support broader rehabilitation goals.

Clinical reasoning is essential. The therapist must consider the patient's presentation, goals, preferences, and response to treatment when deciding how manual therapy fits into care.

When used thoughtfully, manual therapy can be a valuable component of evidence-informed rehabilitation.

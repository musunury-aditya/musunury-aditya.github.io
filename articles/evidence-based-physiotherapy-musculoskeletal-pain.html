The Role of Evidence-Based Physiotherapy in Musculoskeletal Pain Management

May 2025  ·  6 min read  ·  By Aditya Musunury

Musculoskeletal pain is one of the most common reasons patients seek physiotherapy. In clinical practice, successful management begins with a structured assessment, a clear understanding of the patient's goals, and an individualized rehabilitation plan.

Evidence-based physiotherapy integrates current research, clinical reasoning, and patient preferences. In musculoskeletal care, this often includes exercise therapy, education, load management, movement retraining, and selected manual therapy techniques where clinically appropriate.

Exercise is central to long-term recovery. Rather than focusing only on short-term pain relief, physiotherapy aims to improve mobility, strength, tolerance, confidence, and functional capacity in a way that supports daily living and reduces recurrence risk.

Patient education is equally important. When patients understand the nature of pain, tissue recovery, pacing, and realistic progression, adherence often improves and fear-related movement avoidance can decrease.

In modern rehabilitation, evidence-based care does not mean using the same program for everyone. It means choosing the most appropriate interventions for the individual and adjusting them according to response, progress, and long-term functional needs.

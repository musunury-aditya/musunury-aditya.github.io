Home Exercise Programs: Why Adherence Matters for Recovery

March 2025  ·  5 min read  ·  By Aditya Musunury

A home exercise program is often one of the most important parts of physiotherapy. Many outcomes depend on what happens between sessions rather than only during appointments.

Adherence improves when exercises are realistic, clearly explained, and directly connected to the patient's functional goals. Simplicity and relevance matter.

Education is essential. Patients are often more consistent when they understand why a specific exercise was prescribed and what improvement it is intended to support.

Review and progression also matter. A home program should not remain static if the patient is improving or struggling. Ongoing adjustment helps keep it safe, effective, and motivating.

An effective home program should feel achievable, purposeful, and individualized.

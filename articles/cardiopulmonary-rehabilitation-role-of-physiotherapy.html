Cardiopulmonary Rehabilitation and the Expanding Role of Physiotherapy

April 2025  ·  6 min read  ·  By Aditya Musunury

Cardiopulmonary physiotherapy supports patients whose function is affected by respiratory or cardiovascular conditions. The goal is to improve breathing efficiency, endurance, exercise tolerance, and overall participation.

Physiotherapists may use graded activity, breathing control strategies, postural advice, airway clearance techniques, pacing, and supervised progression of functional exercise.

Functional goals are especially important in this area. Patients often want to walk farther, manage stairs, reduce fatigue, and participate more fully in daily life.

As healthcare evolves, physiotherapists continue to play an increasing role in outpatient rehabilitation, home-based care, and interdisciplinary chronic disease support.

Clinical decisions should remain individualized and should always consider exercise tolerance, symptom response, and coordination with the broader care team.
